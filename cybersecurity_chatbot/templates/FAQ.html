Legal Frameworks

What laws does Nexus Bot follow? COMPLIANCE

Nexus Bot is designed to operate in compliance with both local (Myanmar) and international data protection frameworks, prioritizing user privacy and ethical data handling.

Myanmar Laws LOCAL

Law Protecting the Privacy and Security of Citizens (2017, Suspended/Amended 2021): Initially enacted to safeguard citizens' privacy, key protections were suspended in 2021 by the State Administration Council (Law No. 04/2021), enabling broad government access for "security" purposes. To mitigate these risks, we encrypt all user data and minimize retention.

Electronic Transactions Law (2004, Amended 2014, 2021): Regulates digital transactions and allows government oversight for cybersecurity purposes. We only process uploads for security scanning and promptly delete them.

Telecommunications Law (2013, Amended 2017): While this law primarily governs telecom operations and includes government access provisions, we do not request, store, or share telecom data.

Cybersecurity Law (2025): Introduces mandatory data retention and broad government access powers. We actively apply encryption and short retention periods to reduce exposure.

International Laws GLOBAL

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) – EU (2018): We seek explicit user consent for data processing, support data deletion ("right to be forgotten"), and provide breach notifications in line with GDPR requirements.

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) – USA (2020): We do not sell personal data and honor user requests to opt out of non-essential processing.

Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL) – China (2021): We obtain explicit consent for sensitive personal data (e.g., biometric or image data) and anonymize it when possible.

Brazilian General Data Protection Law (LGPD) – Brazil (2020): We respect user rights to access, correct, or delete personal data.

ASEAN Framework on Personal Data Protection: We follow key regional principles such as purpose limitation, data security, and accountability.

How does Nexus Bot protect my privacy? SECURITY

Nexus Bot employs multiple layers of protection to safeguard your privacy:

  • Encryption: All chat messages are encrypted at rest and in transit using industry-standard cryptography.
  • Data Retention: We store data for no longer than 30 days (configurable) before automatic deletion, in line with data minimization best practices.
  • User Consent: Data scanning for security purposes is only performed with your consent at registration, ensuring transparency under GDPR, PIPL, and LGPD.
  • No Data Selling: We do not sell personal data to third parties under any circumstances.
Data Minimization

Nexus Bot follows the principle of data minimization, collecting only what is necessary for functionality and retaining it for the shortest time possible to fulfill its purpose.

What are the risks under local Myanmar laws? RISK

Due to amendments and new legislation in Myanmar (2021 and 2025), authorities may compel access to digital data for "security" reasons. While we reduce this risk through encryption and limited retention, we cannot fully prevent lawful government requests.

Important Notice

We strongly recommend avoiding sensitive personal information in chats. While we employ robust encryption and data minimization practices, no system can completely eliminate the risk of government access requests under current Myanmar legislation.

Technical Implementation

How does encryption work in Nexus Bot? TECH

Nexus Bot employs multiple layers of encryption:

  • In Transit: All communications use TLS 1.3 with perfect forward secrecy
  • At Rest: Data is encrypted using AES-256 encryption
  • End-to-End: Optional end-to-end encryption for sensitive conversations
  • Key Management: Keys are managed using a certified key management system
// Example encryption implementation
function encryptData(data, key) {
  const algorithm = 'aes-256-gcm';
  const iv = crypto.randomBytes(16);
  const cipher = crypto.createCipheriv(algorithm, key, iv);
  let encrypted = cipher.update(data, 'utf8', 'hex');
  encrypted += cipher.final('hex');
  return {
    iv: iv.toString('hex'),
    tag: cipher.getAuthTag().toString('hex'),
    content: encrypted
  };
}
